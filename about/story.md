# 📖 My Story

> *From curious kid to software builder — the long way.*

---

## The Beginning

It started with curiosity — the kind that makes you want to know how things work, not just that they work.

From a young age, I was drawn to the world of hacking and cybersecurity. Not for any harmful reason, but because the idea of understanding systems at a deeper level than most people ever see was genuinely fascinating to me. I started exploring that world early, learning about how networks work, how applications are built, and — more importantly — how they can be broken apart and understood.

That curiosity pulled me into cybersecurity first. And cybersecurity, naturally, pulled me toward programming.

---

## Finding Python

I stumbled into Python the way most self-taught developers do — through open-source projects and random scripts on the internet. I'd find something interesting, read through the code, try to understand what each line was doing, and slowly start modifying it.

There were no courses. No structured learning path. Just GitHub repositories, Stack Overflow threads, and a lot of trial and error.

It took a long time. Longer than it would have with formal education, probably. But learning it the hard way meant I actually understood it — not just memorized syntax, but genuinely understood how to think in Python.

---

## Growing the Skillset

Once Python clicked, things started moving faster. I began writing my own scripts, then tools, then full desktop applications with GUIs. I got into automation — building bots and tools that could do repetitive tasks automatically — and that became my main passion.

I also dove deeper into reverse engineering. Taking Android APKs apart, understanding how they communicate, bypassing SSL pinning to analyze network traffic — all of this became a natural extension of that early curiosity about how things work under the hood.

Eventually I moved into web development too. Learning HTML, CSS, JavaScript, React on the frontend, and Django, Flask, FastAPI, Node.js on the backend. It rounded out my skills and opened up new kinds of projects.

---

## Where I Am Now

I build software under the **Mr-SxR** brand — tools focused on automation, reliability, and doing one thing well. Every release carries the standard that the name represents: **Speciality & Reliability**.

Everything I know, I taught myself. That's not a complaint — it's something I'm proud of. It means every skill I have was earned through genuine effort and persistence.

The journey is still going. There's always more to learn, more to build, more to understand.
